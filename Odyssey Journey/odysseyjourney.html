Odysseus's Journey To The Underworld

Nolan Baglio




Cimmeria

    “And she made the outer limits, the Ocean River’s bounds where Cimmerian people have their homes—their realm and city shrouded in mist and cloud”(Homer 15-16). In The Odyssey, we see a reference to Cimmeria. To learn more about Cimmeria, secondary research helps us get more information. “The real people of Cimmeria (pronounced and sometimes spelt Kimmeria) are almost as cloaked in mystery as their land is in Homer. It is hard to find decent, non-racially motivated or Conan-related material on the internet about the Cimmerians”(Kay). This shows that the people of Cimmeria are true mysteries. I represent this by simply putting two pieces of land with rockier terrain on both sides of the river.

The River Of Oceans

    “The sail stretched taut as she cut the sea all day and the sun sank and the roads of the world grew dark. And she made the outer limits, the Ocean River’s bounds”(Homer 13-15). The Odyssey references that Odysseus must travel by boat in order to get to his destination. Secondary research tells us that he travels on The River of Oceans. “Odysseus journey to the Underworld through the River of Ocean located on the island of the Cimmerians. Here he pours libations and performs sacrifices, pouring blood into a cup to attract the souls to appear”(Clark). The research done shows how the river goes through the island of Cimmeria. To represent this, I put a river in the middle of the model. In addition to this, I used the other references including no sunlight and the boat with the river as well to make it as accurate as possible.

The Boat Of Odysseus

    “Now down we came to the ship at the water’s edge, we hauled and launched her into the sunlit breakers first, stepped the mast in the black craft and set our sail”(Homer 1-3). The first three lines of Book 11 immediately go straight to the idea that Odysseus needs his ship in order to get to this destination. “There, gaining that point, we beached our craft and herding out the sheep, we picked our way by the Ocean’s banks until we gained the place that Circe made our goal”(Homer 22-25). This shows that where he needed to go was only reachable by his boat. The boat is an important part of the story, because it is what helps him along his journey home.

The Land Of No Sunlight

    “The eye of the Sun can never flash his rays through the dark and bring them light, not when he climbs the starry skies or when he wheels back down from the heights to touch the earth once more— an endless, deadly night overhangs those wretched men”(Homer 17-21). This quote supports the idea of how Cimmeria receives no sunlight. The quote describes how the sun can't bring them light, in addition to describing how an endless night overhangs those in Cimmeria. To represent this idea, the land and the river is painted black.

The Sacrifices

    In The Odyssey, we see Odysseus refer to sheep. He says, “and loaded the sheep aboard, the ram and ewe,”(Homer 4). This points to the idea that there were sheep on board, but reading further tells what their purpose was. “I took the victims, over the trench I cut their throats and the dark blood flowed in—and up out of Erebus they came, flocking toward me now, the ghosts of the dead and gone…”(Homer 38-41). This shows that the purpose of the sheep were to be sacrifices, and that sacrificing them allows for the dead to come and for Odysseus to have these exchanges. I put four sheep in the back of the boat to represent this.
